Understanding our workshop environment

Welcome to the OpenShift security workshop! Here you'll be able to practice your prowess responding to a security breach and making security posture improvements in a safe environment.

Let's get started!

1.1 - The workshop scenario

For this hands on workshop, we're going to be an OpenShift team member at ACME Financial Services, a large bank operating in New Zealand. They run a significant number of customer facing workloads on OpenShift and have a small core team of platform engineers supporting the platform.

Throughout this workshop we'll be in the shoes of Sarah, a senior OpenShift Platform Engineer at ACME, a day in the life for her typically sees her handling platform health and maintenance tasks, assisting application teams with feature requests and she is also rostered on to the platform incident management queue on a regular rotation.

1.2 - Understanding the environment

For this workshop you'll be given access to the ACME Financial Services Production OpenShift 4.20 cluster and some supporting tools.

You can and are encouraged to use any supporting documentation or other resources in order to tackle each of the workshop tasks.

ACME Financial Services OpenShift Web Console

1.3 - Obtain your environment

To get underway open your web browser and navigate to this Google Sheet to allocate yourself a workshop environment Sign Up sheet

Put your name next to any unallocated environment and use credentials on that row to log in to the OpenShift Web Console https://console-openshift-console.apps.workshop.sandbox1614.opentlc.com

Workshop registration page

If you have an environment and are logged in to the OpenShift Web Console, you're now ready to move on to Exercise 2, let the challenge begin! 🎉
